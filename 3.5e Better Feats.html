NOTES

Acrobatic, Agile, Alertness, Animal Affinity, Athletic, Deceitful, Deft Hands, Diligent, Investigator, Magical Aptitude, Negotiator, Nimble Fingers, Persuasive, Self-Sufficient, and Stealthy have been replaced with Skill Pair.

Brew Potion, Craft Magic Arms And Armor, Craft Rod, Craft Staff, Craft Wand, Craft Wondrous Item, Forge Ring, and Scribe Scroll have been removed. Anyone of the requisite spellcaster level can craft these items without a feat.

Improved Bull Rush, Improved Disarm, Improved Feint, Improved Overrun, Improved Sunder, Improved Trip have been rolled into Combat Expertise.

Furthermore, these maneuvers only trigger an opportunity attack if you attempt them without holding a non-improvised weapon, even if you don't have Combat Expertise. If you have Improved Unarmed Strike / Martial Arts / Brawler, then you no longer trigger an opportunity attack if you attempt these actions without holding a non-improvised weapon.

Great Fortitude, Iron Will, and Lightning Reflexes have been rolled into Exceptional Saving Throw.

Great Cleave, Greater Spell Focus, Greater Spell Penetration, Greater Two Weapon Fighting, Greater Weapon Focus, Greater Weapon Specialization, Improved Precise Shot, Improved Two-Weapon Fighting, and Tower Shield Proficiency have been rolled into their base forms.

Eschew Materials, Exotic Weapon Proficiency, Improved Familiar, Improved Shield Bash, Leadership, Many Shot, Martial Weapon Proficiency, Natural Spell, Run, Shot on the Run, Simple Weapon Proficiency, Spell Mastery, Spring Attack, and Weapon Finesse have been removed from the game. Many of them are now irrelevant.

Power Attack, Rapid Shot, and Two Weapon Fighting have been rewritten.

SPECIAL FEATS

Power Attack: Applies a damage bonus of +3 / +7 / +12 and an accuracy penalty of -3 to all melee attacks made during the round.

At level 1, the bonus is +3. At level 4, the bonus is +7. At level 8, the bonus is +12.

If you used Critical Strike or Flurry earlier in the round, you cannot use Power Attack.

Flurry: Make an extra melee attack during your turn but applies a -4 / -2 / -0 penalty to all melee attacks made during the round and applies a -4 / -2 / -0 penalty to armor class during the round.

At level 1, the penalty is -4. At level 4, the penalty is -2. At level 8, the penalty is -0.

If you used Critical Strike or Power Attack earlier in the round, you cannot use Flurry.

Critical Strike: Applies a critical threat range multiplier of 2x / 3x / 4x to all melee attacks made during the round but applies a -3 penalty to armor class during the round.

At level 1, the multiplier is 2x. At level 4, the multiplier is 3x. At level 8, the multiplier is 4x.

If you used Flurry or Power Attack earlier in the round, you cannot use Critical Strike.

Deadly Shot, Rapid Shot, and Critical Shot are the ranged equivalents of the above attacks and follow the exact same patterns.

Dueling: If you are wielding one melee weapon and no shield, you gain +1 / +2 / +3 to your attack rolls with the weapon and Armor Class.

At level 1, the bonus is +1. At level 4, the bonus is +2. At level 8, the bonus is +3. The bonus to Armor Class is considered a dodge bonus.

Shield Mastery: Your shield bonuses apply to your Touch Armor Class and are not lost when flat-footed.

Two Weapon Fighting: If you are wielding two melee weapons, your two weapon fighting penalty is reduced from -6 / -10 to -4 / -8, -2 / -6, -0 / -4.

At level 1, the penalty is 4 / -8. At level 4, the penalty is -2 / -6. At level 8, the penalty is -0 / -4. The first penalty is for the main hand attack; the second penalty is for the offhand attack.

As a reminder, wielding two light weapons adds +4 to attack rolls made with the offhand, even if you do not have the feat. If you are capable of making multiple attacks, all additional attacks are made with the main hand; the off hand is only capable of making one attack per turn.

Marksmanship: If you are wielding a ranged weapon, you gain +1 / +2 / +3 to your attack rolls.

At level 1, the bonus is +1. At level 4, the bonus is +2. At level 8, the bonus is +3.

FEATS

Armor Proficiency (Heavy): Become proficient* in heavy armor.

Armor Proficiency (Light): Become proficient* in light armor.

Armor Proficiency (Medium): Become proficient* in medium armor.

Armor Proficiency (Shield): Become proficient* with shields.

*Without armor proficiency, all attack rolls and skill checks involving movement are penalized by the base Armor Class of the armor. Arcane spell failure chance applies as well. With armor proficiency, attack rolls are not penalized, arcane spell failure is removed, and only Balance, Climb, Escape Artist, Hide, Jump, Move Silently, Sleight of Hand, and Tumble checks are affected by armor.

Augment Summoning: Each creature conjured with a summon spell gains a +4 enhancement bonus to Strength and Constitution for the duration of the spell.

For clarity, this increases the modifier of Strength and Constitution by +2.

Blind Fight: In melee, half the chance of missing due to concealment.

An invisible attacker gets no advantages related to hitting you in melee. You do not lose your Dexterity bonus to Armor Class, and the attacker does not get the usual +2 bonus for being invisible. The invisible attacker’s bonuses do still apply for ranged attacks.

Darkness and poor visibility reduces your speed to three-quarters normal, instead of usual one-half.

Cleave: If you kill a character or reduce them to 0 HP, you get a free melee attack against a nearby character.

Combat Casting: You get a +4 bonus on Concentration checks made to cast a spell or use a spell-like ability while taking damage or while you are grappling or pinned.

Combat Expertise: You gain a +4 to all checks made to perform or resist combat maneuvers.

Combat Expertise replaces the following feats: Improved Bull Rush, Improved Disarm, Improved Feint, Improved Overrun, Improved Sunder, Improved Trip

Combat Reflexes: Add your Dexterity bonus to your attack roll when making an opportunity attack. If your Dexterity bonus is already added to your attack roll, add it again. You can make opportunity attacks while flat-footed.

Deflect Arrows: Once per round when you would normally be hit with a ranged weapon, you may deflect it so that you take no damage from it. You must be aware of the attack, not flat-footed, and either have a free hand or be using a shield.

Deflecting a ranged weapon is a free action. Unusually massive ranged weapons and ranged attacks generated by spell effects can’t be deflected.

Die Hard: Immediately stabilize when at 0 Hit Points or less. You do not lose 1 Hit Point per round.

When at 0 Hit Points or less, you can choose to stay up instead of falling unconscious. You can take either a move action or a standard action. You are unable to perform full round actions. Move actions are not penalized, but any strenuous action (standard actions, most reactions, most free actions) will reduce your Hit Points by 1 for each action taken.

If you hit -10 Hit Points, you will die, like you normally would.

Dodge: Gain a +2 dodge bonus to your Armor Class.

Endurance: You gain +4 to any check testing physical endurance. Running long distance, going without food or water, and resisting the effects of hot or cold environments are examples of when the bonus would apply.

Additionally, you can sleep while wearing armor without penalty.

Exceptional Saving Throw: Increase one of your saving throws by +4.

You can take this feat multiple times, but must choose a different saving throw to improve each time. If you have already improved all three of your saving throws, you can no longer take this feat.

Exceptional Saving Throw replaces the following feats: Great Fortitude, Iron Will, Lightning Reflexes.

Extra Turning: Double the amount of turning you can do in one day.

You can take this feat multiple times. Each time you take it after the first, the multiplier increases by one.

Far Shot: The range of all projectile weapons you use is doubled.

Improved Counterspell: When counterspelling, you may use any spell of the same school that is at least one level higher than the target spell.

Normally, you can only counter a spell with the same spell or with a spell specifically designed to counter the target spell.

Improved Critical Threat: Double the critical threat range of all weapons you wield. If another effect doubles the critical threat range already, this feat increases the critical threat multiplier by one.

Improved Critical Hit: Increase the critical damage multiplier by one.

Improved Grapple: You get a +4 bonus on all grapple checks.

Improved Initiative: You get a +4 bonus on initiative checks.

Improved Turning: You turn creatures as if you were one level higher than you are in the class that grants you the ability.

Improved Unarmed Strike / Martial Arts / Brawler: You are considered to be armed even when unarmed; you do not provoke opportunity attacks from armed opponents when you attack them while unarmed. However, you still get an opportunity attack against any opponent who makes an unarmed attack on you.

In addition, your unarmed strikes can deal lethal or nonlethal damage, at your discretion.

Mobility: You get a +4 dodge bonus to Armor Class against opportunity attacks caused when you move into or out of a threatened area. A condition that makes you lose your Dexterity bonus to Armor Class (if any) also makes you lose dodge bonuses.

Dodge bonuses stack with each other, unlike most types of bonuses.

Mounted Combat, Mounted Archery, Mounted Ride By, Mounted Spirited Charge, Mounted Trample: WIP

Point Blank Shot: You get a +2 bonus on attack and damage rolls with ranged weapon attacks made within close range.

Precise Shot: You ignore the -4 penalty for firing into a melee. You ignore defense bonuses provided from cover (except total cover) or concealment (except for total concealment). You automatically hit a target that is grappling.

Quick Draw: You draw a weapon as a free action. You draw a hidden weapon as a move action. You can now make full attacks with thrown weapons.

Rapid Reload: You reload crossbows and other large ranged weapons as a free action. You can now make full attacks with crossbows and large ranged weapons.

Skill Focus: Choose a skill. You get a +3 bonus on all checks involving that skill. This feat can be selected multiple times, but a different skill must be selected each time.

Skill Pair: Choose two skills. You get a +2 bonus to all checks involving those skills. This feat can be selected multiple times, but a different pair of skills must be selected each time.

Snatch Arrow: When using the Deflect Arrows feat, you may catch the projectile instead of just deflecting it. Thrown weapons can immediately be thrown back at the original attacker (even though it isn’t your turn) as a free action or kept for later use.

You must have at least one hand free (holding nothing) to use this feat.

Spell Focus: Choose a school of magic. You add +2 to the Difficulty Class of spells you cast from the selected school of magic.

Spell Penetration: You get +4 to checks made to overcome spell resistance.

Stunning Fist: You must declare that you are using this feat before you make your attack roll (thus, a failed attack roll ruins the attempt). Stunning Fist forces a foe damaged by your unarmed attack to make a Fortitude saving throw (DC 10 + ½ your character level + your Wis modifier), in addition to dealing damage normally. A defender who fails this saving throw is stunned for 1 round (until just before your next action). A stunned creature drops everything held, can’t take actions, takes a -2 penalty to Armor Class, and loses his Dexterity bonus to Armor Class. You may attempt a stunning attack once per day for every four levels you have attained, and no more than once per round. Constructs, oozes, plants, undead, incorporeal creatures, and creatures immune to critical hits cannot be stunned.

A monk who selects this feat may attempt a stunning attack a number of times per day equal to her monk level, plus one more time per day for every four levels she has in classes other than monk.

Toughness: Reduce all damage taken by 2.

Track: https://www.d20srd.org/srd/feats.htm#track

Beginning at 8th level, move at normal speed while following tracks without taking the normal -5 penalty. Move at up to twice normal speed while tracking but only take a -10 penalty instead of the normal -20.

Two Weapon Defense: You get a +2 shield bonus to your Armor Class while wielding two weapons.

Weapon Focus: You get +2 attack to a specific class of weapon.

Weapon class definitions are flexible; bladed weapons, large weapons, and polearms are all valid ways of categorizing weapons, despite their overlap.

Weapon Specialization: You get +4 damage to a specific class of weapon.

Weapon class definitions are flexible; bladed weapons, large weapons, and polearms are all valid ways of categorizing weapons, despite their overlap.

Whirlwind Attack: Make a full attack. Instead of making your normal amount of attacks, attack every opponent within reach.
